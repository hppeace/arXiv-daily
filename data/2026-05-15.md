### [1] [Breaking Global Self-Attention Bottlenecks in Transformer-based Spiking Neural Networks with Local Structure-Aware Self-Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13887)
*Lingdong Li, Hangming Zhang, Qiang Yu*

#### 🧩 TL;DR
本文提出LSFormer，一种基于Transformer的脉冲神经网络，通过引入Spiking Response Pooling（SPooling）和Local Structure-Aware Spiking Self-Attention（LS-SSA），解决了现有Transformer-SNN中最大池化特征保留不足和全局自注意力计算冗余的问题，在Tiny-ImageNet和N-CALTECH101上分别超越基线4.3%和8.6%的分类准确率。

---

#### 📘 Detailed Summary
**Motivation:** 现有基于Transformer的脉冲神经网络（SNNs）存在两个关键缺陷：一是使用最大池化层降低特征图尺寸时，仅保留最强响应而无法全面表征代表性区域特征；二是全局自注意力机制涉及所有全局特征交互，导致计算冗余和二次复杂度，与SNN固有的稀疏性和能效特性相矛盾。

**Method:** LSFormer提出两个核心模块：Spiking Response Pooling（SPooling）替代最大池化以更全面地保留区域特征，以及Local Structure-Aware Spiking Self-Attention（LS-SSA），首次在Transformer-SNN中引入局部膨胀窗口机制，同时捕捉局部细节和长距离依赖关系。

**Result:** LSFormer在多个基准上达到当前最优性能，尤其在更具挑战性的静态数据集Tiny-ImageNet上top-1分类准确率领先基线4.3%，在神经形态数据集N-CALTECH101上领先8.6%。

**Conclusion:** LSFormer展示了将能效脉冲模型推向大规模视觉任务实际部署的潜力，证明了在Transformer-SNN中兼顾局部结构与全局依赖的设计策略能够显著提升性能。

---

#### 📄 Abstract
Transformer-based Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) integrate SNNs with global self-attention and have demonstrated impressive performance. However, existing Transformer-based SNNs suffer from two fundamental limitations. First, they typically employ max pooling layers to reduce the size of feature maps, but the max pooling captures only the strongest response and fails to comprehensively preserve representative regional features. Second, the global self-attention involves all global feature interactions, resulting in computational redundancy and quadratic computational complexity, thus conflicting with the sparse and energy-efficient characteristics of SNNs. To address these challenges, we develop Local Structure-Aware Spiking Transformer (LSFormer), a novel Transformer-based Spiking Neural Network that incorporates Spiking Response Pooling (SPooling) and Local Structure-Aware Spiking Self-Attention (LS-SSA). For the first time, our LSFormer leverages a local dilated window mechanism to capture both local details and long-range dependencies. Experimental results demonstrate that our LSFormer achieves state-of-the-art performance compared to existing advanced Transformer-based SNNs. Notably, on the more challenging static dataset Tiny-ImageNet and neuromorphic dataset N-CALTECH101, LSFormer substantially outperforms state-of-the-art baselines by 4.3\% and 8.6\% in top-1 classification accuracy, respectively. These results highlight the potential of LSFormer to advance energy-efficient spiking models toward practical deployment in large-scale vision applications.


### [2] [Neuromorphic Graph Anomaly Detection via Adaptive STDP and Spiking Graph Neural Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13863)
*Abdul Joseph Fofanah, Lian Wen, David Chen, Tsungcheng Yao, Kwabena Sarpong*

#### 🧩 TL;DR
本文提出ASTDP-GAD，一种融合脉冲图神经网络和STDP学习的自适应脉冲时序动态可塑性框架，用于动态网络中的图异常检测，在保持生物合理性和能效的同时，在九个数据集上实现了优于现有方法的检测精度。

---

#### 📘 Detailed Summary
**Motivation:** 现有动态网络异常检测方法在能效、时间精度和自适应性方面存在不足，缺乏兼顾生物合理性、能量高效且能捕捉动态网络中因果时序关系的统一检测框架。

**Method:** ASTDP-GAD框架融合了脉冲神经计算、STDP学习和图异常检测，核心创新包括：自适应LIF动态的时序脉冲图编码、基于侧抑制的LIF图注意力机制、基于STDP原型更新的事件驱动超图记忆、基于脉冲不规则性的脉冲率对比池化、捕获因果时序关系的自适应STDP层，以及多尺度时序卷积与多因素异常融合。

**Result:** 在九个动态和静态图数据集上的广泛实验表明，该方法在异常检测精度上优于现有方法；理论分析提供了严格保证，包括信息保持的脉冲编码、连续注意力函数逼近、最优原型收敛、可证明的异常选择界、STDP稳定收敛以及多因素融合方差缩减最高达5倍。

**Conclusion:** ASTDP-GAD为动态图异常检测提供了一种兼具生物合理性、能量高效性和理论可保证性的神经形态计算方案，适用于网络安全和工业监控等领域的低功耗部署需求。

---

#### 📄 Abstract
Anomaly detection in dynamic networks is critical for applications from cybersecurity to industrial monitoring, yet existing methods face challenges in energy efficiency, temporal precision, and adaptability. This paper introduces ASTDP-GAD, a novel Adaptive Spiking Temporal Dynamics Plasticity framework for Graph Anomaly Detection that integrates spiking graph neural networks with STDP learning for energy-efficient neuromorphic detection in dynamic networks. Our framework unifies spiking neural computation, STDP learning, and graph-based anomaly detection through the following key innovations: temporal spike graph encoding with adaptive Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) dynamics; LIF-based graph attention with lateral inhibition; event-driven hypergraph memory with STDP-inspired prototype updates; spike rate contrast pooling based on spiking irregularity; adaptive STDP layers capturing causal temporal relationships; and multi-scale temporal convolution with multi-factor anomaly fusion. Theoretical analysis provides rigorous guarantees: spike encoding preserves input information with resolution scaling linearly in simulation steps and hidden dimension; LIFGAT approximates any continuous attention function; hypergraph memory converges to optimal prototypes; contrast pooling achieves provable anomaly selection bounds; STDP learning converges stably; and multi-factor fusion produces calibrated scores with up to $5\times$ variance reduction. Extensive experiments on nine datasets on both dynamic and static graphs demonstrate superior anomaly detection accuracy while maintaining biological plausibility and energy efficiency for neuromorphic deployment.


### [3] [NeuroTrain: Surveying Local Learning Rules for Spiking Neural Networks with an Open Benchmarking Framework](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15058)
*Alessio Caviglia, Filippo Marostica, Roberta Bardini, Alessandro Savino, Stefano Di Carlo*

#### 🧩 TL;DR
该综述提出了一个系统性的SNN训练算法分类体系，涵盖代理梯度反向传播、局部与三因子学习规则、生物启发可塑性机制、ANN-to-SNN转换及非标准优化策略，并发布了基于snnTorch的开源统一框架NeuroTrain，以支持可重复的标准化基准测试。

---

#### 📘 Detailed Summary
**Motivation:** 当前脉冲神经网络（SNN）训练算法迅速发展，涌现出大量在生物启发性、计算结构和硬件适应性上各异的算法，但领域内缺乏一个统一的、细粒度的分类体系来系统梳理这些方法并阐明它们之间的概念关系，导致文献碎片化且难以进行公平比较。

**Method:** 该综述构建了一个涵盖代理梯度反向传播、局部与三因子学习规则、生物启发可塑性机制、ANN-to-SNN转换及非标准优化策略的细粒度SNN训练算法分类体系；同时发布了NeuroTrain——一个基于snnTorch的开源模块化框架，实现了代表性算法并支持跨数据集、架构和训练体制的一致基准测试。

**Result:** 从计算原理、学习信号和局部性三个维度对每类算法进行了系统分析；通过NeuroTrain框架整合了碎片化文献并提供了可复用的基准测试平台，但摘要中未给出具体的数值性能指标或对比结果。

**Conclusion:** 该研究识别了SNN训练算法中的共有模式，指出了当前面临的开放性挑战，并勾勒了面向可扩展、高效SNN训练的未来研究方向；其分类体系和开源框架为领域内的标准化比较与算法演进提供了重要基础。

---

#### 📄 Abstract
The rapid expansion of spiking neural networks (SNNs) has led to a proliferation of training algorithms that differ widely in biological inspiration, computational structure, and hardware suitability. Despite this progress, the field lacks a unified, fine-grained taxonomy that systematically organizes these approaches and clarifies their conceptual relationships. This survey provides a comprehensive taxonomy of SNN training algorithms, spanning surrogate-gradient backpropagation, local and three-factor learning rules, biologically inspired plasticity mechanisms, ANN-to-SNN conversion pipelines, and non-standard optimization strategies. We analyze each class in terms of its computational principles, learning signals, and locality properties. To support reproducible research, we release NeuroTrain, an open-source snnTorch-based framework that implements a representative set of these algorithms within a unified, modular, and extendable framework, enabling consistent benchmarking across datasets, architectures, and training regimes. By consolidating fragmented literature and providing a reusable benchmarking framework, this survey identifies common patterns, highlights open challenges, and outlines promising directions for future work on scalable, efficient SNN training.
