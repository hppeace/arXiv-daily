[1] EdgeSpike: Spiking Neural Networks for Low-Power Autonomous Sensing in Edge IoT Architectures

Gustav Olaf Yunus Laitinen-Fredriksson Lundstrom-Imanov, Taner Yilmaz

🧩 TL;DR

EdgeSpike是一个面向边缘IoT的脉冲神经网络（SNN）协同设计框架，在5类感知任务、3种硬件目标上平均准确率91.4%（距INT8 CNN基线仅1.2个百分点），在神经形态硬件上能效提升18-47倍（平均31倍），在Cortex-M上提升4.6-7.9倍（平均6.1倍），并通过7个月64节点无线部署验证电池寿命延长6.3倍。

📘 Detailed Summary

Motivation: 现有SNN在边缘IoT部署中面临训练效率低、硬件适配困难、缺乏统一端到端框架的问题，且缺乏在多种真实传感任务和异构硬件（神经形态芯片与通用MCU）上的系统性验证与长期现场部署评估。

Method: EdgeSpike提出四方面协同设计：（i）混合代理梯度和直接编码的训练流程；（ii）基于每推理能量和内存预算约束的硬件感知神经架构搜索（NAS）；（iii）针对Intel Loihi 2、SpiNNaker 2和ARM Cortex-M的定制事件驱动运行时，含尖峰稀疏SIMD内核；（iv）无需反向传播的轻量局部可塑性规则，支持持续在设备端适应。

Result: 在关键词识别、振动故障检测、表面肌电图手势识别、77 GHz雷达人体活动分类和声发射结构健康监测5类任务上，平均准确率91.4%，距INT8 CNN基线（92.6%）仅1.2个百分点；神经形态硬件能耗降低18-47倍（平均31x），Cortex-M降低4.6-7.9倍（平均6.1x）；15个任务-硬件配置的端到端延迟均≤9.4 ms；7个月64节点现场部署中，电池寿命从312天延至1978天（6.3x），有设备内适应时季节性漂移下准确率衰减0.7个百分点（无适应时为2.1个百分点）；NAS评估8400个候选架构，获得12点Pareto前沿。

Conclusion: EdgeSpike证明了SNN在边缘IoT低功耗感知中可在接近CNN精度的前提下实现数量级能效提升，且通过硬件感知NAS和局部可塑性规则支持跨硬件部署与持续适应，为自主边缘智能系统提供了统一、可复现的基准框架。

📄 Abstract

We propose EdgeSpike, a co-designed spiking neural network (SNN) framework for autonomous low-power sensing in edge Internet of Things (IoT) architectures. EdgeSpike unifies (i) a hybrid surrogate-gradient and direct-encoding training pipeline, (ii) a hardware-aware neural architecture search (NAS) bounded by per-inference energy and memory budgets, (iii) an event-driven runtime targeting Intel Loihi 2, SpiNNaker 2, and commodity ARM Cortex-M microcontrollers with custom spike-sparse SIMD kernels, and (iv) a lightweight local plasticity rule enabling continual on-device adaptation without backpropagation. The framework is evaluated across five sensing tasks (keyword spotting, vibration-based machine fault detection, surface electromyography gesture recognition, 77 GHz radar human-activity classification, and structural-health acoustic-emission monitoring) on three hardware targets. EdgeSpike achieves a mean classification accuracy of 91.4%, within 1.2 percentage points (pp) of strong INT8 convolutional neural network (CNN) baselines (mean 92.6%), while reducing energy per inference by 18x to 47x on neuromorphic hardware (mean 31x) and by 4.6x to 7.9x on Cortex-M (mean 6.1x). End-to-end latency remains at or below 9.4 ms across all 15 task-hardware configurations. A seven-month, 64-node wireless field deployment confirms a 6.3x extension in projected battery lifetime (from 312 to 1978 days at 2 Wh per node) and bounded accuracy degradation under seasonal drift (0.7 pp with on-device adaptation versus 2.1 pp without). Hardware-aware NAS evaluates 8400 candidates and yields a 12-point Pareto front. EdgeSpike will be released as open source with reproducible training pipelines, hardware-portable runtimes, and benchmark suites.

[2] Binary Spiking Neural Networks as Causal Models

Aditya Kar, Emiliano Lorini, Timothée Masquelier

🧩 TL;DR

本文提出一种基于因果模型与逻辑求解器的二值脉冲神经网络（BSNN）解释方法，通过SAT/SMT求解器计算溯因解释，保证解释中不包含无关特征，优于SHAP方法。

📘 Detailed Summary

Motivation: 现有可解释AI方法（如SHAP）无法保证所生成的解释中不含完全无关的特征，缺乏严格的形式化保证；同时，脉冲神经网络的二值动态特性尚未被充分用于生成具有逻辑可验证性的解释。

Method: 本文对二值脉冲神经网络（BSNN）进行因果分析，将其脉冲活动形式化为二值因果模型，并利用SAT（布尔可满足性问题）求解器和SMT（可满足性模理论）求解器从该因果模型中计算溯因解释（abductive explanations），从而解释网络的分类决策。

Result: 在MNIST数据集上训练的BSNN上，SAT和SMT方法均成功生成了基于像素级特征的溯因解释；与SHAP对比，本文方法保证生成的解释中不包含任何完全无关的特征，而SHAP无法提供此类保证。

Conclusion: 基于因果表示与逻辑求解器的解释方法能够在二值脉冲神经网络中提供具有形式保证的溯因解释，消除了无关特征对解释的干扰，为脉冲神经网络的可靠可解释性提供了新途径。

📄 Abstract

We provide a causal analysis of Binary Spiking Neural Networks (BSNNs) to explain their behavior. We formally define a BSNN and represent its spiking activity as a binary causal model. Thanks to this causal representation, we are able to explain the output of the network by leveraging logic-based methods. In particular, we show that we can successfully use a SAT as well as a SMT solver to compute abductive explanations from this binary causal model. To illustrate our approach, we trained the BSNN on the standard MNIST dataset and applied our SAT-based and SMT-based methods to finding abductive explanations of the network's classifications based on pixel-level features. We also compared the found explanations against SHAP, a popular method used in the area of explainable AI. We show that, unlike SHAP, our approach guarantees that a found explanation does not contain completely irrelevant features.
