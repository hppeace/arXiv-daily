### [1] [EventGeM: Global-to-Local Feature Matching for Event-Based Visual Place Recognition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05807)
*Adam D. Hines, Gokul B. Nair, Nicolás Marticorena, Michael Milford, Tobias Fischer*

#### 🧩 TL;DR
本文提出了EventGeM，一种用于事件相机视觉位置识别的最先进全局到局部特征融合管道，通过结合预训练的视觉Transformer、MaxViT和视觉基础模型，在多个基准数据集上实现了最先进的定位性能，同时保持实时运行能力。

---

#### 📘 Detailed Summary
**Motivation:** 事件相机因其稀疏激活和高时间分辨率在机器人视觉任务中日益流行，但在视觉位置识别任务中，现有方法在准确性和实时性方面仍有改进空间。本研究旨在开发一种能够融合全局与局部特征的事件相机视觉位置识别系统，以在多种光照条件下实现高精度实时定位。

**Method:** EventGeM采用全局到局部特征融合管道，首先使用预训练的ViT-S/16骨干网络从事件直方图图像中提取全局特征进行初始匹配预测，然后使用预训练的MaxViT骨干网络检测局部特征关键点，结合RANSAC进行基于2D单应性的重排序。为进一步优化，还使用预训练的视觉基础模型进行深度估计，以比较参考图像与查询图像之间的结构相似性。

**Result:** 该方法在多个基准数据集和不同光照条件下均实现了最先进的定位性能，超越了当前最佳的事件相机位置识别方法。系统能够在各种计算架构上实时运行，并在真实机器人平台上成功部署，实现了基于事件流的在线定位。

**Conclusion:** EventGeM展示了事件相机视觉位置识别的强大潜力，通过融合多尺度特征和深度信息实现了高精度实时定位。该方法为事件相机在机器人导航和定位任务中的实际应用提供了有效解决方案，特别是在对能量效率和实时性要求较高的场景中具有重要价值。

---

#### 📄 Abstract
Dynamic vision sensors, also known as event cameras, are rapidly rising in popularity for robotic and computer vision tasks due to their sparse activation and high-temporal resolution. Event cameras have been used in robotic navigation and localization tasks where accurate positioning needs to occur on small and frequent time scales, or when energy concerns are paramount. In this work, we present EventGeM, a state-of-the-art global to local feature fusion pipeline for event-based Visual Place Recognition. We use a pre-trained vision transformer (ViT-S/16) backbone to obtain global feature patch for initial match predictions embeddings from event histogram images. Local feature keypoints were then detected using a pre-trained MaxViT backbone for 2D-homography based re-ranking with RANSAC. For additional re-ranking refinement, we subsequently used a pre-trained vision foundation model for depth estimation to compare structural similarity between references and queries. Our work performs state-of-the-art localization when compared to the best currently available event-based place recognition method across several benchmark datasets and lighting conditions all whilst being fully capable of running in real-time when deployed across a variety of compute architectures. We demonstrate the capability of EventGeM in a real-world deployment on a robotic platform for online localization using event streams directly from an event camera. Project page: https://eventgemvpr.github.io/


### [2] [Low-latency Event-based Object Detection with Spatially-Sparse Linear Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06228)
*Haiqing Hao, Zhipeng Sui, Rong Zou, Zijia Dai, Nikola Zubić, Davide Scaramuzza, Wenhui Wang*

#### 🧩 TL;DR
本文提出了空间稀疏线性注意力（SSLA）及其在事件相机目标检测中的应用SSLA-Det，通过引入混合空间状态分解和散射-计算-聚集训练过程，在保持并行训练效率的同时实现状态级稀疏性，显著降低了每事件计算量。

---

#### 📘 Detailed Summary
**Motivation:** 现有异步事件神经网络虽然能实现低延迟的事件级预测更新，但仍面临两个瓶颈：循环架构难以在长序列上高效训练，而提高准确性通常会增加每事件计算量和延迟。标准线性注意力虽然支持并行训练和循环推理，但为每个事件更新全局状态会导致准确性-效率权衡不佳，这在需要细粒度表示的目标检测中尤为突出。

**Method:** 本文提出了空间稀疏线性注意力（SSLA），通过引入混合空间状态分解和散射-计算-聚集训练过程，在保持高效并行训练的同时实现状态级稀疏性。基于SSLA构建了端到端的异步线性注意力模型SSLA-Det，专门用于事件相机目标检测，该架构充分利用了事件数据的空间稀疏性特征。

**Result:** 在Gen1和N-Caltech101数据集上，SSLA-Det在异步方法中达到了最先进的准确性，分别获得0.375 mAP和0.515 mAP。与先前最强的异步基线相比，每事件计算量减少了20倍以上，显著提升了准确性-效率权衡。

**Conclusion:** 该研究证明了线性注意力在低延迟事件视觉任务中的潜力，通过状态级稀疏性设计有效解决了事件相机目标检测中的计算效率问题。SSLA框架为异步事件处理提供了新的设计范式，平衡了训练并行性、推理效率和模型准确性，为实时事件视觉应用开辟了新的可能性。

---

#### 📄 Abstract
Event cameras provide sequential visual data with spatial sparsity and high temporal resolution, making them attractive for low-latency object detection. Existing asynchronous event-based neural networks realize this low-latency advantage by updating predictions event-by-event, but still suffer from two bottlenecks: recurrent architectures are difficult to train efficiently on long sequences, and improving accuracy often increases per-event computation and latency. Linear attention is appealing in this setting because it supports parallel training and recurrent inference. However, standard linear attention updates a global state for every event, yielding a poor accuracy-efficiency trade-off, which is problematic for object detection, where fine-grained representations and thus states are preferred. The key challenge is therefore to introduce sparse state activation that exploits event sparsity while preserving efficient parallel training. We propose Spatially-Sparse Linear Attention (SSLA), which introduces a mixture-of-spaces state decomposition and a scatter-compute-gather training procedure, enabling state-level sparsity as well as training parallelism. Built on SSLA, we develop an end-to-end asynchronous linear attention model, SSLA-Det, for event-based object detection. On Gen1 and N-Caltech101, SSLA-Det achieves state-of-the-art accuracy among asynchronous methods, reaching 0.375 mAP and 0.515 mAP, respectively, while reducing per-event computation by more than 20 times compared to the strongest prior asynchronous baseline, demonstrating the potential of linear attention for low-latency event-based vision.


### [3] [ODD-SEC: Onboard Drone Detection with a Spinning Event Camera](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06265)
*Kuan Dai, Hongxin Zhang, Sheng Zhong, Yi Zhou*

#### 🧩 TL;DR
本文提出了一种用于移动载体的实时无人机检测系统，采用旋转事件相机实现360°水平视场和方位估计，通过无需运动补偿的图像式事件表示和轻量级神经网络架构，在挑战性条件下实现了可靠检测。

---

#### 📘 Detailed Summary
**Motivation:** 现有基于事件相机的无人机检测方案大多假设相机静止，这极大地限制了其在移动载体（如四足机器人或无人地面车辆）现场作业中的适用性，而传统帧相机系统在快速移动目标或不良光照条件下存在根本性限制。

**Method:** 系统采用旋转事件相机提供360°水平视场，提出了一种无需运动补偿的新型图像式事件表示方法，结合轻量级神经网络架构进行高效时空学习，并在机载Jetson Orin NX平台上实现实时运行。

**Result:** 户外实验结果表明，在挑战性条件下系统实现了可靠检测，平均角度误差低于2°，验证了其在实际监控应用中的适用性，系统能够在移动载体上实时运行。

**Conclusion:** 该研究证明了基于事件相机的无人机检测系统在移动载体上的可行性，通过创新的表示方法和轻量架构解决了动态场景下的检测挑战，为实际监控应用提供了有效解决方案，作者承诺开源完整流程以支持未来研究。

---

#### 📄 Abstract
The rapid proliferation of drones requires balancing innovation with regulation. To address security and privacy concerns, techniques for drone detection have attracted significant attention.Passive solutions, such as frame camera-based systems, offer versatility and energy efficiency under typical conditions but are fundamentally constrained by their operational principles in scenarios involving fast-moving targets or adverse illumination.Inspired by biological vision, event cameras asynchronously detect per-pixel brightness changes, offering high dynamic range and microsecond-level responsiveness that make them uniquely suited for drone detection in conditions beyond the reach of conventional frame-based cameras.However, the design of most existing event-based solutions assumes a static camera, greatly limiting their applicability to moving carriers--such as quadrupedal robots or unmanned ground vehicles--during field operations.In this paper, we introduce a real-time drone detection system designed for deployment on moving carriers. The system utilizes a spinning event-based camera, providing a 360° horizontal field of view and enabling bearing estimation of detected drones. A key contribution is a novel image-like event representation that operates without motion compensation, coupled with a lightweight neural network architecture for efficient spatiotemporal learning. Implemented on an onboard Jetson Orin NX, the system can operate in real time. Outdoor experimental results validate reliable detection with a mean angular error below 2° under challenging conditions, underscoring its suitability for real-world surveillance applications. We will open-source our complete pipeline to support future research.
