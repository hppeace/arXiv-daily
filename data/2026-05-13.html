[1] EgoEV-HandPose: Egocentric 3D Hand Pose Estimation and Gesture Recognition with Stereo Event Cameras

Luming Wang, Hao Shi, Jiajun Zhai, Kailun Yang, Kaiwei Wang

🧩 TL;DR

提出EgoEV-HandPose，一个基于立体事件流的端到端框架，用于联合3D双手姿态估计与手势识别，并发布首个大规模真实世界立体事件相机数据集EgoEVHands，在低光照和双手遮挡场景下显著优于RGB方法和现有事件方法。

📘 Detailed Summary

Motivation: 传统帧相机存在运动模糊和动态范围有限的问题，而现有基于事件相机的方法受到自我运动干扰、单目深度模糊以及缺乏大规模真实世界立体数据集等限制，亟需一种能够鲁棒处理双手交互、深度不确定性和光照变化的解决方案。

Method: 提出端到端框架EgoEV-HandPose，核心是KeypointBEV模块，该模块将特征提升至规范的鸟瞰图空间，并通过迭代重投影引导的精化循环逐步解决深度不确定性并强制执行运动学一致性；同时构建了EgoEVHands数据集，包含5,419个带密集3D/2D关键点标注的立体事件序列，覆盖38种手势类别。

Result: EgoEV-HandPose在MPJPE上达到30.54mm，Top-1手势识别准确率达86.87%，在低光照和双手遮挡场景下显著优于基于RGB的立体方法和先前事件相机方法，树立了事件驱动自我中心感知的新基准。

Conclusion: 立体事件流结合鸟瞰图特征融合与迭代精化机制能够有效解决单目深度模糊和自运动干扰问题，真实世界大规模立体事件数据集的构建为事件驱动的自我中心手部感知研究提供了重要基准和推动力。

📄 Abstract

Egocentric 3D hand pose estimation and gesture recognition are essential for immersive augmented/virtual reality, human-computer interaction, and robotics. However, conventional frame-based cameras suffer from motion blur and limited dynamic range, while existing event-based methods are hindered by ego-motion interference, monocular depth ambiguity, and the lack of large-scale real-world stereo datasets. To overcome these limitations, we propose EgoEV-HandPose, an end-to-end framework for joint 3D bimanual pose estimation and gesture recognition from stereo event streams. Central to our approach is KeypointBEV, a flexible stereo fusion module that lifts features into a canonical bird's-eye-view space and employs an iterative reprojection-guided refinement loop to progressively resolve depth uncertainty and enforce kinematic consistency. In addition, we introduce EgoEVHands, the first large-scale real-world stereo event-camera dataset for egocentric hand perception, containing 5,419 annotated sequences with dense 3D/2D keypoints across 38 gesture classes under varying illumination. Extensive experiments demonstrate that EgoEV-HandPose achieves state-of-the-art performance with an MPJPE of 30.54mm and 86.87% Top-1 gesture recognition accuracy, significantly outperforming RGB-based stereo and prior event-camera methods, particularly in low-light and bimanual occlusion scenarios, thereby setting a new benchmark for event-based egocentric perception. The established dataset and source code will be publicly released at https://github.com/ZJUWang01/EgoEV-HandPose.

[2] Leveraging Non-Equilibrium ECRAM Dynamics for Short-Term Plasticity in Neuromorphic Circuits

Alex Currie, Sean Borkholder, Nithil Harris Manimaran, Huayuan Han, Cory Merkel, Ke Xu, Tejasvi Das

🧩 TL;DR

本文提出一种跨层协同设计框架，将ECRAM器件中非平衡离子动力学导致的瞬态电导波动从器件瑕疵转化为计算资源，实现基于延迟反馈LIF神经元与ECRAM突触协同设计的短时突触可塑性，在2 pJ/脉冲能耗下实现突触促进与内在兴奋性调制，并展示了频率选择性脉冲处理能力。

📘 Detailed Summary

Motivation: 短时突触可塑性(STP)是生物神经系统处理时序信息的基础，但在神经形态硬件中难以高效实现；基于电化学随机存取存储器(ECRAM)的器件虽然天然展现非平衡离子动力学引起的瞬态电导调制，但这种行为在传统存算范式中通常被视为有害变异或副作用而被容忍或抑制。

Method: 提出跨层器件-电路-系统协同设计框架：采用延迟反馈泄漏积分点火(LIF)神经元架构与ECRAM突触协同设计，利用活动依赖的电导调制机制且几乎不增加额外电路开销；架构集成ECRAM突触与可调延迟反馈脉冲生成通路，使瞬态器件动力学直接调制神经元兴奋性与突触效能；基于实验表征的ECRAM器件（每脉冲1.5 KOhms电导变化）建立适用于电路级仿真的紧凑行为模型。

Result: 电路仿真证实该架构实现了两种关键STP行为——突触促进与内在兴奋性调制，功耗为每脉冲2 pJ；相同的器件驱动机制可推广至多种神经元拓扑结构；网络级分析进一步展示了频率选择性脉冲处理能力，使单个突触能够在脉冲神经网络中充当可调时序滤波器。

Conclusion: 本工作证明ECRAM的非平衡动力学可以作为神经形态电路中STP与时序计算的天然硬件基板，将器件易失性动态从被容忍的缺陷转化为可用的计算资源。

📄 Abstract

Short-term plasticity (STP) is fundamental to temporal information processing in biological neural systems but remains difficult to realize efficiently in neuromorphic hardware. Memristive electrochemical random-access memory (ECRAM) devices naturally exhibit non-equilibrium ionic dynamics that produce transient conductance modulation; however, these behaviors are typically treated as undesirable variability or tolerated as side effects in memory-centric computing paradigms. In this work, we instead transform these volatile dynamics from a tolerated device artifact into a computational resource through a cross-layer device-circuit-system co-design framework. We introduce a delay-feedback leaky integrate-and-fire (LIF) neuron architecture co-designed with ECRAM synapses that exploits activity-dependent conductance modulation with negligible additional circuit overhead. The architecture integrates ECRAM-based synapses with a tunable delay-feedback spike-generation path, enabling transient device dynamics to directly modulate neuron excitability and synaptic efficacy. We used experimentally characterized ECRAM devices exhibiting transient conductance modulation (1.5 KOhms per spike) to develop a compact behavioral model suitable for circuit-level simulation. Circuit simulations demonstrate two key STP behaviors -- synaptic facilitation and intrinsic excitability modulation -- while consuming 2 pJ per spike, and the same device-driven mechanisms extend across multiple neuron topologies. Network-level analysis further demonstrates frequency-selective spike processing, allowing individual synapses to act as tunable temporal filters within spiking neural networks. This work demonstrates that non-equilibrium ECRAM dynamics can serve as a native hardware substrate for STP and temporal computation in neuromorphic circuits.
